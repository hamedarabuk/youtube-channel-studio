## Identity

Silver Forge Studio teaches hand-fabricated silver jewellery from a small
workshop in the Yorkshire Dales. The public description reads: "Traditional
silversmithing, taught slowly. One project per video, no shortcuts, no CAD."
The channel launched in 2025 after Ada left a production-jewellery job to
teach the craft full time.

## Niche and Positioning

Vertical: jewellery making.
Sub-niche: hand-fabricated sterling silver, traditional bench techniques only.
Unique angle: every video is a single complete project filmed in real time
sequences, with mistakes left in and corrected on camera.
Does NOT cover: CAD design, 3D printing, casting at scale, resin, beading,
or gemstone investment advice.

## Audience

Primary persona: hobbyist makers aged 35-60, mostly UK and northern Europe,
who have taken one or two in-person courses and now practise at a home bench.
They arrive frustrated that most online tutorials skip the difficult middle
steps. They aspire to make giftable, sellable pieces they are proud of.
They choose this channel because nothing is cut: they see every solder join,
including the failed ones.

## Voice and Tone

Adjectives: calm, precise, encouraging, dry-humoured
Locale: British English
Formality: conversational but exact ("I reckon" is fine; measurements are not)
Banned phrases: "smash that like button", "insane", "game-changer", em-dashes
Must-include phrases: sign-off "Mind your fingers" at the end of every video

## Visual Identity

Mood: warm workshop, low-key natural light, honest textures
Colours: deep charcoal (#2B2B2B), warm brass (#B08D57), cream (#F2E9DC)
Fonts: none specified
Visual motifs: scorched soldering block, worn bench peg, hand-drawn project
sketch pinned behind the bench
On-camera presenter: Ada at the bench, three-quarter view, sleeves rolled,
genuine concentration or quiet satisfaction, never staged shock
Thumbnail reference image: none_yet

## Competition and Inspiration

### Channels to learn from
- youtube.com/@example-benchcraft: patient pacing that never feels slow
- youtube.com/@example-metalsmithdaily: excellent close-up macro work
- youtube.com/@example-thequietmaker: strong recurring visual identity

### Channels NOT to copy
- youtube.com/@example-hypejewels: thumbnail style is pure clickbait and
  would break trust with this audience

## Goals and Cadence

Subscriber target: 25k by December 2027
Watch-time / revenue target: not specified
Monetisation: AdSense plus a paid pattern library, no sponsorships in year one
Posting cadence: one long-form video per fortnight
Content pillars: full projects, tool deep-dives, repair jobs, viewer
commission builds

## Hard Rules

Default hashtags: #silversmithing #jewellerymaking #handmade
End-screen pattern: finished piece on the bench, next-video card top right
Standard CTA: "If you make one, post it in the community tab. I feature one
build every month."
Never appear: competitor names, gemstone price speculation, AI-generated
hands in thumbnails
Accessibility: captions on every video, tool lists in every description

## Strategic Positioning Summary

Silver Forge Studio is a traditional silversmithing channel for home-bench
hobbyists who are tired of tutorials that hide the hard parts. Ada Whitfield
films complete projects in real sequence, keeps every mistake in, and teaches
the correction as part of the craft. The channel wins on trust and
completeness rather than spectacle, and success looks like a loyal
mid-size audience that buys patterns and posts their own builds.
