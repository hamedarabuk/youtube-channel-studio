# Channel Foundation Template

This is the structure the `yt-channel-init` skill produces for each channel. It lives at `channels/<your-handle>/foundation.md` and is read as strategic context by the per-video optimisation skill. You do not edit this template directly; it is a reference shape only.

## Identity
- Channel name
- Handle (e.g. @MyChannel)
- URL
- Public channel description
- Founded year
- Creator name
- On-camera or off-camera role

## Niche and positioning
- Vertical (e.g. cooking, gardening, AI tutorials)
- Sub-niche (your specific angle)
- Unique angle versus the rest of the field
- What this channel will not cover

## Audience
- Primary persona: demographic (age, location) and psychographic (interests, values)
- Pain points the audience has
- Aspirations the audience holds
- Why this specific channel earns their attention

## Voice and tone
- Three to five adjectives describing the voice
- Language locale (UK English, US English, etc.)
- Formality level
- Banned phrases or words
- Must-include phrases
- Cultural or regional context

## Visual identity
- Mood (cinematic, raw, editorial, etc.)
- Brand palette (3 to 5 hex colours)
- Fonts (if any)
- Recurring visual motifs
- Main on-camera person and how they should appear in thumbnails
- Character reference image path (if available)

## Competition and inspiration
- Five competitor channels with URLs and what to admire about each
- Three channels to avoid copying with reasons

## Goals and cadence
- Subscriber target with timeline
- Watch-time target
- Monetisation strategy
- Posting cadence (e.g. one long-form per week plus daily Shorts)
- Three to five content pillars

## Hard rules
- Must-include hashtags
- End-screen pattern
- CTA pattern
- Anything that should never appear (banned content, words, themes)
- Accessibility rules (captions, descriptions for visually impaired, etc.)

## What already works (optional)
- Three examples of titles and descriptions that performed well

## Strategic positioning summary
A three to four sentence synthesis of the channel's positioning, written in third person so the optimisation skill can quote it directly when reasoning about new videos.
